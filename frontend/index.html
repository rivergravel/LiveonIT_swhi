Why this exists

The 20-minute neighbourhood idea is simple

🚶

Walking-time routes

Not straight-line distance — actual pedestrian routes calculated via open routing, so the minutes you see are the minutes you'll walk.

📍

Six essential services

Supermarkets, pharmacies, health clinics, bus stops, train stations, and post offices — the daily errands that shape how liveable a street really is.

🌏

Open data, no tracking

Built on OpenStreetMap, Nominatim, and Valhalla. No accounts, no analytics cookies, no data collection. Just type and see.

How it works

Three steps, no signup

1

Type your address

Start typing any Melbourne street address. Suggestions appear as you go — pick one from the dropdown.

2

We search 800 metres around you

The app queries OpenStreetMap for six categories of essential services within an 800-metre radius of your front door.

3

See your walking times

Each service gets a real pedestrian route. You'll see the closest option per category, the walk time in minutes, and the path drawn on the map.

Check your neighbourhood

It takes about ten seconds. No signup required.
